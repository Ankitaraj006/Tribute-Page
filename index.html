Hellen Keller
    Here are timeline of Hellen Keller's life:
  • 1880- Born on June 27 in the small town of Tuscumbia, Alabama
  • 1881- Stricken with an illness that left her without sight or hearing.
  • 1887- Helen's teacher, Anne Sullivan, came to live with the family in Alabama and turned Helen's world around.
  • 1888- In May, Helen went north to visit Boston with her mother and teacher. She spent some time studying at the Perkins Institute for the Blind, and quickly befriended the other blind girls who were her age.
  • 1890- Miss Sullivan took her to the Horace Mann School to begin learning with Miss Sarah Fuller
  • 1892- In winter,Helen seemingly inspired by the beautiful fall foliage around her, she wrote a story called "The Frost King," and sent it up to her teacher at the Perkins Institute as a gift.
  • 1894- Helen attended the Wright-Humanson School for the Deaf in New York City.
  • 1896- She began her studies at the Cambridge School for Young Ladies in Massachusetts.
  • 1889- She successfully qualified for Radcliffe, and entered college in the fall of 1900.
  • 1932- Thanks to Helen's work, braille became the standard system around the world. Helen Keller pushed the United States government for more assistance to the blind.
  • 1955- Helen received an Academy Award for the documentary about her life, Helen Keller in Her Story (originally called The Unconquered).
  • 1968- On June 1, Helen Keller dies in Westport, Connecticut, at the age of 87.
